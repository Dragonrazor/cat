Cats

Cats are animals that are cute and intelligent animals. They have good sense of observation and can mimic human behaviour such as opening doors, opening windows or recognizing sounds that are associated with the owner.

Cats have been domesticated since the beginning of recorded civilization, noted for their keen sense of hunting at night, especially for rats and other rodents. Eventually cats became part of the household and enjoyed a position within a family. Cats are considered to be solitary, however, cats are as social as dogs but they only operate on their own terms, not the owner.

Cats are very specialized hunters. Their sense of balance is excellent and they have no fear of heights. They can jump up many times their height and with their powerful hind legs reaching heights of more than 2m standing still. They have long whiskers that are very sensitive to touch, thus cats can crawl through the narrowest gaps because of the lack of shoulder bones and flexible attachments between bones. Cats also have fat pads on their paws that transmit information to the cat and the tongue can also be used as another means to understand the environment. Lastly, cats have ears that can move to a specific sound and can hear the tinest movement of mice, well beyond human range.
